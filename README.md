# The Safety Lamp Vault

Description:

The Safety Lamp Vault is a mobile application for collectors of mining antiquities, historians of subterranean engineering, and keepers of coalfield heritage, dedicated to cataloging the vintage flame safety lamps and carbide cap lamps that illuminated the deepest pits and detected deadly gases before the era of portable electric LEDs and digital multi-gas detectors.

Systematize your collection of the "beacons of the underworld"—from classic brass Davy lamps that used wire gauze to prevent explosions to steel Clanny and Wolf patterns, from brass hand-cranked miners' dial compasses to water-fed calcium carbide miners' lamps with polished reflectors.

Create a digital archive of underground history by recording the ventilation airflow compatibility, wick mechanisms, fuel specifications, era, and the specific colliery or mining district each specimen protected.

## Example Object Fields (The "Subterranean Artifact" Model):

Vault Control Number: Unique database code (e.g., "SLV-WOLF-1912-PA-089")

Apparatus Classification: Category (Flame Safety Lamp, Carbide Cap Lamp, Oil Wick Cap Lamp, Pocket Dial, Surveyor's Transit Compass).

Foundry or Manufacturer: Company name or patent holder (Wolf Safety Lamp Co., Koehler, Justrite, Thomas & Williams, Ackroyd & Best).

Gauze Configuration: Type and mesh density of the safety wire mesh—critical for flame lamps (Single gauze, double gauze, iron mesh, copper mesh for explosive environments).

Fuel & Illuminant Requirements: What the lamp burns to operate (Naptha, whale oil, lard oil, calcium carbide pellets, kerosene).

Locking Mechanism Type: The vintage security system used to prevent miners from opening the lamp underground and sparking an explosion (Magnetic lock, lead seal lock, rivet lock, screw lock).

Air-Inflow Design: How the lamp draws oxygen to test for gases (Bottom-feed air ring, top-feed tubes, deflector ring).

Body Metal & Metallurgy: Primary construction materials (Polished brass, stamped steel, cast aluminum, copper trim).

Physical Proportions: Total height to the top of the bonnet hoop, reflector diameter, and dry weight.

Preservation Status: Working condition, glass cylinder integrity (cracked/pristine), flint igniter functionality, original patina.

Accompanying Gear: Original magnetic unlocking keys, tip cleaners for carbide burners, original leather mounting straps, or spare glass blanks.

Inspector & Colliery Stamps: Hard-stamped numbers showing mine inspector approvals, railroad ownership, or specific coal company inventory tags.

Colliery Provenance: Historical context (Appalachian coal fields, Welsh valleys, Ruhr valley mines, decommissioned silver mine, estate auction).
